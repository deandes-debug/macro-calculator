About This Calculator

MacroFitCalc uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, one of the most accurate methods for estimating Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).

Why Mifflin-St Jeor?

A 1990 study published in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition found the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to be more accurate than the older Harris-Benedict equation (1919). The study showed that Mifflin-St Jeor predicted within 10% of measured resting energy expenditure for most individuals.

Primary Reference: Mifflin MD, St Jeor ST, Hill LA, Scott BJ, Daugherty SA, Koh YO. A new predictive equation for resting energy expenditure in healthy individuals. Am J Clin Nutr. 1990;51(2):241-7.

How We Calculate Macros

Your macronutrient distribution varies based on your goal:

  • Weight Loss: 2.0g protein per kg bodyweight to preserve muscle during fat loss. Calories 20% below TDEE.
  • Maintenance: 1.6g protein per kg bodyweight with balanced distribution. Calories match TDEE.
  • Muscle Gain: 1.8g protein per kg bodyweight with higher carbs for training fuel. Calories 10% above TDEE.

Important: This calculator provides estimates for educational purposes only. Individual nutritional needs vary based on metabolism, genetics, health conditions, and other factors. Always consult with a registered dietitian or healthcare provider before making significant dietary changes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my protein so high?

For fat loss, we recommend 2.0g/kg bodyweight to preserve muscle mass during your calorie deficit. Research shows this is optimal for maintaining strength while losing fat. For muscle gain, 1.8g/kg supports growth, and for maintenance, 1.6g/kg is sufficient.

Is this calculator accurate?

The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is one of the most accurate formulas for estimating caloric needs, with studies showing it predicts within 10% of measured energy expenditure for most people. However, individual results may vary based on factors like muscle mass, metabolism, and genetics.

Do I need to hit these numbers exactly?

Protein is most important (¬±5g is fine). Carbs and fats can be more flexible as long as total calories stay consistent. Focus on hitting protein and total calories; the rest will follow.

Why use this over MyFitnessPal or other calculators?

Unlike most calculators, we don't store your data, show ads, or track you across the web. All calculations happen locally in your browser. Plus, our goal-specific macro ratios are based on current research for optimizing body composition.

How do I actually hit these macros?

Track your food using an app like MyFitnessPal or Cronometer. Weigh your food with a kitchen scale for accuracy. Focus on whole foods like lean meats, vegetables, whole grains, and healthy fats. Meal prep helps ensure you hit your targets consistently.

What if I'm not losing/gaining weight as expected?

Formulas provide estimates, not guarantees. If you're not seeing results after 3-4 weeks of consistent tracking, adjust calories by 10% (decrease for fat loss, increase for muscle gain) and monitor for another 2-3 weeks.

Should I adjust my macros over time?

Yes. As you lose or gain weight, your caloric needs change. Recalculate every 10-15 pounds of weight change, or if you notice your progress stalling for 2-3 weeks.
