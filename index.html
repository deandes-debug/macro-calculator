Frequently Asked Questions

Transparency about our methodology and accuracy

How accurate is this calculator?

We use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (published 1990 in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition), which is accurate within ¬±10% for most people. This is the same formula used by MyFitnessPal, Precision Nutrition, and recommended by the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics.

Individual variance exists due to genetics, muscle mass, metabolic adaptation, and activity accuracy. We recommend tracking results for 2-3 weeks and adjusting by ¬±100-200 calories if needed.

What formula do you use?

Do you store my health data?

No. All calculations happen in your browser using JavaScript. Your age, weight, height, and other inputs never leave your device. We don't have servers that collect this data. If you click "Save Results," data is stored in your browser's local storage only (not on our servers). See our Privacy Policy for details.

How does this compare to other calculators?

Our BMR calculations match MyFitnessPal, IIFYM.com, and Precision Nutrition Calculator exactly (we've tested with identical inputs). We use the same Mifflin-St Jeor formula. The difference with other calculators is in macro splits and meal planning features, which we've designed to be more flexible and educational.

Can I verify your calculations?

Absolutely! When you calculate your macros, we show you the complete formula breakdown with your actual numbers plugged in. You can verify each step yourself or compare against any other calculator. We believe in complete transparency‚Äîthat's why we show all the math, not just the final numbers.
