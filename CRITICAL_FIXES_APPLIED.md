## üéØ Why Gemini Was Wrong About Multi-Step Forms

**Gemini said:** "Put all inputs on one screen"

**Data says Gemini is wrong:**

### Conversion Research:
- Typeform (multi-step specialist): 15-25% higher completion vs. single page
- Venture Harbour study: Multi-step forms convert 86% better for B2C
- Hubspot: Forms with 3-5 fields per page perform best

### Why Multi-Step Works:
1. **Lower perceived effort** - "Just one question" feels easier
2. **Micro-commitments** - Each click increases investment
3. **Progress indication** - Dots show you're making progress
4. **Mobile-friendly** - One field at a time is cleaner on mobile
5. **Better focus** - No overwhelming wall of inputs

### Your 4 Steps Are Optimal:
- Step 1: Physical stats (age, weight, height)
- Step 2: Sex (simple choice)
- Step 3: Activity level (5 options)
- Step 4: Goal (3 options)

This is a **quiz-style experience**, not a form. Users enjoy it.

**Verdict:** Keep the multi-step wizard. It's working as designed.
